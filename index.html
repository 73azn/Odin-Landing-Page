Discover Al Madinah

Al Madinah, or Medina, is a historic city in Saudi Arabia and the second holiest city in Islam. It’s known for the Prophet's Mosque and its significant religious history

Where to go ?

The Prophet’s Mosque is situated in Medina, Saudi Arabia. It was established by the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) and is the second holiest site in Islam. It contains the Prophet’s tomb and is a major site of pilgrimage, especially during the Hajj and Umrah

The Rawdat al-Sharifa is a sacred area within the Prophet’s Mosque (Al-Masjid an-Nabawi) in Medina, specifically located between the Prophet Muhammad’s (peace be upon him) pulpit (minbar) and his blessed tomb. It is considered one of the holiest places in Islam after the Sacred Mosque (Al-Masjid al-Haram) and the Prophet’s Mosque itself

Quba Mosque is located in the outskirts of Medina. It is the first mosque built in the history of Islam and was established by the Prophet Muhammad (pbuh) and his companions when they migrated from Mecca to Medina

Martyrs’ Mosque, also known as Masjid al-Shuhada, is located in the vicinity of Uhud Mountain near Medina. It is named in honor of the martyrs of the Battle of Uhud, which took place in 3 AH (After Hijra) between the early Muslims and the Quraysh of Mecca
